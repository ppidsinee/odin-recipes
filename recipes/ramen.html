Pork Chashu Ramen

Home made juicy pork chashu ramen with simple ingrediant and does not required a whole day of cooking

Ingrediennts

For tare

  • Pork belly
  • Dry Shitake mushroom
  • Kombu
  • Bonito flakes (optional)
  • Soy sauce
  • Mirin
  • Water

For the ramen

  • Udon
  • Oat milk
  • Seaweed
  • Green onion (optional)

    • For the marinated egg

    • Eggs
    • Mirin
    • Soya sauce
    • Water
    • Dry chilli (if you need a special kicküëåüèΩ)

    Directions

    Night before eating

    Pork Chashuü•©

    1. Clean the pork belly and tap with kitchen towel to dry
    2. In a sauce pan add soya sauce, mirin, water, kombu and bonito flakes. Wait until boiling
    3. Add pork belly and dry Shitake mushroom into the pot wait until boil
    4. Recude the heat to medium and continue cooking for an hours
    5. Once cook remove the pan from stove and wait until cool down
    6. Move the pork belly and sauce into a container and refridgerate overnight for a juicy umami ü§§

    Marinated eggs ü•ö

    1. Boil egg for 11 minutes
    2. Put them into cold water and wait for 3 minutes before peeling
    3. In a sauce pan, add soya sauve, mirin, water and dry chilly and cook until boild
    4. Move the sauce into container, wait until cold and add eggs
    5. Refridgerate overnight

    Day of eating

    1. Remove pork chashu from the sauce
    2. Warm the Tare on a sauce pan
    3. Cut the pork chashu and warm it on a hot pan
    4. Put soya milk on a sauce pan
    5. Put Udon into a ramen bowl
    6. Pour Tare over the Udon and followed with soya milk
    7. Add cutted pork chashu and egg on top
    8. Voila! Enjoy your juicy ramen!
